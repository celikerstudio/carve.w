## Target Audience

**Primary persona:** Tech-savvy mensen (devs, designers, tech workers) die ook fit willen worden of blijven. Ze waarderen de AI/tech angle en herkennen de "building in public" journey.

**Why they follow:**
- Curious hoe AI fitness kan verbeteren
- Herkennen de struggle van fit blijven met een desk job
- Geinteresseerd in indie dev journey
- Willen een app die niet cringe of bro-science is

**Their journey:** Volgen → Engaged → carve.wiki bezoeken → Download

---

## The Angle

**Who you are:** Dev die een fitness app bouwt met AI. Geen fitness guru, gewoon iemand die tech en training combineert.

### Content Pillars

1. **Dev updates** (40%) — Behind-the-scenes of building Carve
2. **Fitness journey** (30%) — Your own progress, POV gym content
3. **Tips & fun facts** (20%) — Things you learn along the way
4. **AI showcases** (10%) — Showing what AI can do (transparent, not deceptive)
5. **Anime training inspo** — 1 per 4-6 posts (bonus pillar)

### What makes you different
- Honest about not being a fitness expert
- Meta glasses for unique POV content
- Tech/AI angle in fitness space
- Building in public
- Solo backpacking through Asia while developing

---

## Golden Rules

1. **Consistency > Frequency** — 1 post/week for a year beats daily for a month
2. **Real > Polished** — Amateur vibe builds trust
3. **Share > Show off** — "Here's what I learned" not "Look how great I am"
4. **Sustainable > Viral** — This is a 5-year game, not a sprint
5. **No burnout** — If it's not fun, slow down
6. **Quality of connection > follower count** — 100 engaged fans > 10K passive followers

---

## Filming Rules (Meta Glasses)

Conservatieve aanpak om issues te voorkomen:

- **Geen gezichten** van anderen in beeld
- **Rustige hoeken** van de gym, vermijd drukke zones
- **Eigen rek/station** — film alleen waar jij traint
- **Bij twijfel, niet filmen** — liever missen dan problemen
- **AI content labelen** — altijd duidelijk maken wat AI-generated is

> De meeste content kan ook thuis of buiten. Gym POV is nice-to-have, niet must-have.

## 5-Year Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundation (Year 1)
*"Just start, build the habit"*

**Platform:** Instagram only + Reddit
**Frequency:** 1-2 posts per week

**Focus:**
- Finding your voice and style
- Documenting the Carve journey
- Getting comfortable with content creation
- Building a small but engaged following
- Aim for ~50-100 posts by end of year

**Tools:** Meta glasses, phone, CapCut, Claude/ChatGPT for captions, Meta Business Suite

**Milestones:**
- [ ] First post published
- [ ] First 10 posts
- [ ] First 100 followers
- [ ] First post with 1K+ views
- [ ] 3 months consistent posting
- [ ] 6 months consistent posting
- [ ] 50 posts total
- [ ] 500 followers

---

### Phase 2: Cross-Platform (Year 2)
*"Repurpose, don't create twice"*

**Platforms:** Instagram + TikTok
**Frequency:** 2-3 posts per week

**Focus:**
- Same Reels go to TikTok (minimal extra work)
- Learn what performs differently on each platform
- Start building email list via carve.wiki
- First collaborations with small fitness/dev accounts

**Milestones:**
- [ ] TikTok account created and active
- [ ] 1,000 followers on Instagram
- [ ] 500 followers on TikTok
- [ ] Email list started (100+ subscribers)
- [ ] First collab with another creator

---

### Phase 3: Depth (Year 3)
*"Go deeper, not wider"*

**Platforms:** Instagram, TikTok, + YouTube Shorts + Twitter/X
**Frequency:** 3-4 posts per week (mostly repurposing)

**Focus:**
- Experiment with longer-form content
- User stories from Carve users
- AI tutorials and deep-dives
- Dev community engagement on Twitter/X

**Milestones:**
- [ ] 5,000 combined followers
- [ ] YouTube channel started
- [ ] First paid collaboration or sponsorship
- [ ] Carve featured in press/blog/podcast
- [ ] Content that drives actual app downloads

---

### Phase 4: Community (Year 4)
*"From audience to community"*

**Platforms:** All previous + community platform
**Frequency:** Flexible, community-driven

**Focus:**
- Building a real community around Carve
- User-generated content
- Challenges and events
- Less "me" content, more community content

**Milestones:**
- [ ] 10,000+ combined followers
- [ ] Active community (Discord, in-app, or otherwise)
- [ ] User-generated content flowing naturally
- [ ] Community events/challenges running

---

### Phase 5: Established (Year 5)
*"Cruise control"*

**Focus:**
- Sustainable rhythm that fits your life
- Carve sells itself through reputation
- Post when inspired, not obligated
- Delegate where possible

**Signs of Success:**
- [ ] Carve is recognized in fitness app space
- [ ] Social media supports the business, not drains you
- [ ] You still enjoy creating content
- [ ] Can take breaks without losing momentum

## Planned Platforms

### TikTok (Year 2, ~December 2025)
- Cross-post Instagram Reels (minimal extra work)
- Adjust captions for TikTok style
- Learn what performs differently vs Instagram
- Same content pillars as Instagram

### YouTube (Year 3, ~December 2026)
- **Shorts:** Cross-post Reels/TikToks
- **Long-form (optional):** Dev vlogs, tutorials, app deep-dives
- Focus on searchable content (tutorials, how-tos)

### Twitter/X (Year 3, ~December 2026)
- Dev community / indie hacker engagement
- Building in public updates
- Tech/AI discussions
- Less polished, more conversational

### Facebook (On hold)
- Only if Instagram audience asks for it
- Reels auto-post to Facebook (Meta integration)
- Lower priority than all other platforms
